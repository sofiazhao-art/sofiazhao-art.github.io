Statement

My practice centers on capturing the tense interaction between the subject and its environment, exploring moments in which chaos and order, fragmentation and wholeness are on the verge of imbalance, yet have not fully collapsed. I try to locate an unstable space at the edge between abstraction and figuration. Within this space, the body no longer appears as a complete or clearly defined image, but rather as a trace of experience—fragmented, reassembled, and left behind after external regulation, the intervention of others, and collisions with the surrounding environment.

For me, the painting is a site of events in which color generates potential energy. Through contrasts of density, directional brushwork, a sense of speed, the shapes of color fields, and layers of accumulation, I create a sense of compression, engulfment, and intrusion exerted by the environment upon the subject. Fragmented and distorted limbs, compressed spaces, and a continuously flowing sense of unease recur throughout my work.

I choose to weaken narrative not in order to detach emotion, but to allow viewers to confront more directly the visual impact and psychological pressure produced by incomplete bodies and instability. Painting, for me, is not a way to explain the unconscious, but a process through which unspoken questions within inner experience gradually emerge. I hope the work can ultimately become an open space of viewing, where vitality suddenly erupts in the moment of encounter, in a way that feels profound and almost primal.

我的创作核心在于捕捉主体与环境之间那种紧绷的相互作用，去探索混乱与秩序、破碎与完整之间将要失衡却又尚未彻底坍塌的瞬间。 我试图在抽象与具象的边缘找到一种不稳定的空间。在这个空间里，身体不再是完整、清晰的形象，而更像是在外部规训、 他者介入以及与环境碰撞之后，留下的分裂、重组和残余的经验痕迹。

对我而言，画面是一个由色彩制造势能的事件现场。我会通过密度对比、笔触方向、速度感、色块形状和层层叠压， 去营造环境对主体的挤压、裹挟与侵入。在我的作品中，肢体的碎裂与变形、空间的压迫感以及某种持续流动的不安，反复出现。

我选择弱化叙事，不是为了抽离情感，而是为了让观看者更直接地面对残缺肢体和不稳定性所带来的视觉冲击与精神压力。 绘画对我来说并不是去解释潜意识，而是让内部经验中那些难以言说的问题慢慢浮现。我希望作品最终成为一个开放的观看空间， 让生命力以一种深刻且近乎原始的方式，在观看的瞬间突然爆发出来。
